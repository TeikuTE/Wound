The project in one paragraph
WOUND is a solo journaling tabletop RPG duology: two short books bound together (or rendered as one PDF) that argue with each other across a mid-book pivot. Book 1 (Temporal Echoes, 100 pages) is by Dr. Eleanor Chen — a careful, clinical scientist trying to teach players how to preserve themselves through time displacement. Book 2 (emiT, 80 pages) is by Alan Rose — a 340-year survivor arguing that preservation is impossible and the point is to transform. They were once colleagues. They no longer speak. The book is the argument they're having across the pages.
The reader experiences this as two visually distinct halves of a single book. The aesthetic difference between halves is the design's primary job.
The non-negotiables (Tier 1, minimum viable)
Even if everything else fails, this must be true:

Book 1 is light. Book 2 is dark.

Book 1: black text on cream or white pages
Book 2: white text on near-black pages (#0A0A0A, not pure #000000 — pure black is too harsh for long reading)


Book 1 uses serif type. Book 2 uses monospace type.

Book 1: EB Garamond for body, any clean serif display face for headings
Book 2: IBM Plex Mono for everything — body and display


The voice attribution markers [CHEN] and [ALAN] are visually distinct.

In Book 1, [ALAN] intrusions should feel like they don't belong on the page
In Book 2, [CHEN] intrusions should feel like they don't belong on the page
At minimum: different typeface from surrounding body text, possibly different alignment or weight


Mechanics are scannable.

All dice notation, target numbers, modifiers, and difficulty values are bolded
Numbered procedural steps render as numbered lists, not prose
Tables stay tables, not paragraphs


The reader can physically tell which book they're in within one second of opening to any page.

That's the floor. A reader could play the game off Tier 1 alone, and the books would still feel like deliberate counterparts rather than two halves of the same document.
The recommended target (Tier 2, V1 ship target)
Build everything in Tier 1, plus:
Book 1 — Dr. Chen's manual
Visual reference register: A late-Victorian / early-20th-century scientific manual, updated. Think Candela Obscura by Darrington Press crossed with NASA technical documentation from the 1960s. Careful. Annotated. Built by someone who believes documentation is a moral act.
Specifics:

Page color: Warm cream (#FBFAF6 or close). Not white. Cream signals paper that has aged but been carefully preserved.
Body text: EB Garamond, ~10.5pt, generous leading (~14pt). Justified or left-aligned ragged-right both acceptable; designer's call based on what looks better with the page proportions.
Display type: Playfair Display (or Cormorant if Playfair feels too modern) for chapter titles and major subsection heads.
Page proportions: 6×9" or A5. Single column for body text is fine for V1 — two-column is Tier 3.
Margins: Generous outer margin, especially on the side that will hold marginalia. ~1.25" outer, ~0.75" inner, 0.75" top/bottom.
Margin notes from Chen herself: Italic EB Garamond, slightly smaller (~9pt), set in the outer margin, separated from body by a thin rule. These are Chen's own annotations on her own text.
Margin intrusions from Alan: Caveat font (handwritten script), set at a slight irregular angle if achievable, in the outer margin or interleaved between paragraphs. These should feel wrong on the page — like someone with a pen has defaced a careful manuscript. The handwriting can overlap a rule, slightly cross into the body text margin, or be set crookedly. The wrongness is the point.
Mechanical elements (rolls, mods, dice): Bolded in EB Garamond. No separate mechbox containers — mechanics flow inline with the prose, with typographic emphasis doing the visual lifting.
Latin chapter epigraphs: Set in italic small caps, centered, with a horizontal rule above. They feel like archival headers.

Book 2 — Alan's manual
Visual reference register: Cy_Borg by Stockholm Kartell. Mothership by Tuesday Knight Games. A terminal that was supposed to be decommissioned three centuries ago. A document typed by someone who has stopped pretending typography matters.
Specifics:

Page color: Near-black (#0A0A0A). Not pure black.
Body text: IBM Plex Mono, ~9.5pt, slightly looser leading than Chen's book (~14pt-15pt). White (#F5F5F5, not pure white — slight off-white is easier on the eyes against the dark background). Left-aligned ragged-right. Never justified — justified mono looks awful.
Display type: Also IBM Plex Mono, just larger and bolder. Alan doesn't get to switch fonts for emphasis. He doesn't have that luxury.
Page proportions: Same as Book 1 (matching dimensions are important — they're bound as one book).
Margins: Same as Book 1, but the marginalia behavior reverses.
Headers and chapter titles: Set in monospace caps, possibly with a single ASCII-style horizontal rule made of ─ characters or - repeated. They look like terminal output, not typeset prose.
Margin notes from Alan himself: IBM Plex Mono italic, slightly smaller, set in the outer margin. These are Alan's annotations on his own text.
Margin intrusions from Chen: IBM Plex Mono in a lighter weight — making her voice look like older typewriter strikes that didn't ink fully on the page, or like she's typing on a machine that's failing. Same font as everything else around it, but visually subordinate. The clash isn't tonal contrast (he has none); it's that her surgical precision can't make it through his system intact.
Mechanical elements: Same bolding system as Book 1 — flowed inline, no callout boxes.
No Latin epigraphs. Alan's chapters open with the existing prose.

The shared elements

Chapter heading hierarchy is identical across both books (so the reader's brain knows what's a chapter vs a subsection regardless of which half they're in). Just visually expressed in different vocabularies.
Tables in both books: clean, ruled, scannable. Chen's tables get thin rules and serif numerics. Alan's tables get a single rule above and below with monospace contents — terminal-style.
Mechboxes do not exist. No callout containers. No tinted boxes. Mechanics live inline with the prose.
Page numbers are footer-bottom-outside, consistently positioned.
Headers show the chapter title on each spread (running heads). This is for usability mid-session.

The pivot (the Interlude)
There is one Interlude between Book 1 and Book 2. It should be visually distinct from both books — neither cream nor near-black. White page, black text, a different typeface (a clean transitional sans like Söhne, Inter, or Source Sans Pro). This is the journalist who found both manuscripts speaking briefly in his own voice. Set it apart so it reads as a pivot point, not a continuation of either book.
The Interlude is also where the visual contract reverses. After the Interlude, the reader is in Alan's world.
Tier 3 — Kickstarter / final edition
Save these for the second printing or stretch goals:

Two-column body text in Book 1 (Chen would set her text in columns; she's organized)
Single-column slightly narrower in Book 2 (Alan would not bother organizing)
Custom Faction iconography for the four Factions (Church Templar, Loop Authority, Scionfall, Chronoclasts) appearing on relevant pages
The four MEMO documents in Appendix E styled as individual found-document artifacts (different stationery, different typewriters, real watermarks)
The Saul Files (Appendix H) when written, presented as recovered evidence — file numbers, redactions, photographs of pages
Custom oracle table illustration (a single decorative element per oracle category)
Hand-drawn equations in Chen's marginalia for the Harmonic Codex (Appendix A)
Designed title pages for each book (rather than typeset)
A wordless map of the Chronoverse on the inside cover or as endpapers

None of this is required to ship V1.
What you don't need to design

Cover. Separate brief. Provide separately.
itch.io storefront banner. Separate asset.
Backmatter. Standard credits page format.
Appendix H content. Currently a placeholder; will be filled later.

Critical readability constraints
The book is a tabletop RPG manual. Players will reference it mid-session, scanning for rules. Aesthetic must not interfere with readability:

Body text minimum 9.5pt in Plex Mono, 10.5pt in EB Garamond
Line length 60-75 characters maximum (Plex Mono at smaller size handles narrower)
Tables must be tables (not flowed prose)
Dice notation and target numbers must be bolded and visible at a glance
Procedural steps must be numbered and on separate lines
The "atmosphere" pages (chapter openers, the Interlude, the MEMO documents) can be more formally adventurous because no one needs to find a rule on those pages

If at any point the aesthetic and readability fight, readability wins. This is a rulebook before it's an art object.
Deliverables for V1

Two paragraph style libraries (one per book) for use in Affinity Publisher
Two master spreads (one per book) with marginalia gutters configured
One Interlude spread style
One sample chapter laid out in each book's style (Chapter 1 of each is fine) — used as the template for the rest
PDF proof of the sample chapters before committing to full-book layout


Notes on simplification
If the designer needs to cut for time, cut in this order:

First: drop the Caveat handwriting intrusions in Chen's book. Replace with italic EB Garamond margin notes attributed — AR. The voice still comes through; just less viscerally.
Second: drop the "lighter weight Plex Mono" trick for Chen's intrusions in Alan's book. Replace with italic Plex Mono. Same logic.
Third: drop the cream paper color. White is fine if rendering on screen.
Fourth: drop the Latin epigraphs styling — they can be plain italic centered text.

The order matters. The handwriting is the most expensive element and the least essential. The page color difference is the cheapest dramatic effect to keep.
What stays even in the most aggressive cut: black text on white for Book 1, white text on near-black for Book 2, serif body for Book 1, monospace body for Book 2, distinct voice markers, bolded mechanics. That's the floor and it's enough to ship.

# ⬡ WOUND — Design System

> *"Time is not an ocean or a river. It's a broken machine. You don't drift—you short-circuit."*
> — Alan Rose, *emiT*

---

## What this is

**WOUND** is a solo journaling RPG **duology** sold as a single PDF on itch.io (~$10). Two books bound back-to-back in a single file:

- **Book 1 — *Temporal Echoes*** (Dr. Eleanor Chen). Warm parchment, Victorian scientific journal, academic grief.
- **Book 2 — *emiT*** (Alan Rose). Brutalist zine, black-background terminal output, clinical void.

The reader physically crosses the spine from one world into another. The tension is **architectural** — it lives in the binding and the reader's hands. Neither book softens toward the other. The only place they merge is the cover.

The product is a high-fidelity web application built strictly for **`@media print` PDF export** at **digest size (5.5" × 8.5")**.

### The core visual signature: **The Intrusion System**
The most important design element in the book. Each narrator breaks into the other's pages:

- **Alan → Chen:** frantic *Caveat* scrawl, dark void-colored rectangles cutting into warm parchment. Signed "—AR". Rotates -1.5° to -2.5°.
- **Chen → Alan:** clinical *IBM Plex Mono* on warm parchment "taped" to his cold void. Header cataloged by his system: `Dr. Eleanor Chen // [DATE UNKNOWN]`. Rotates +1° to +2°.
- **Alan's own fragments** (in his book): Plex Mono Light, floating, rotated -4° to +5°. Broadcasting to no one.
- **Chen's own marginalia** (in her book): Caveat, `--el-annotation` brown, faint connecting lines to referenced text.

## CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS — How the copy is written

WOUND is a **two-voice document**. Every word you write belongs to either Chen, Alan, Zaaken, or the neutral system layer (rules text for the reader). Getting the voice wrong is worse than getting the visuals wrong.

### Dr. Eleanor Chen (Book 1)
- **Voice:** academic, warm, meticulous, increasingly grieving. She is a researcher documenting a phenomenon she loves. She uses **first-person plural** ("we observe," "we postulate") when she's being a scientist; she slides into **first-person singular** when grief leaks through.
- **Casing:** Title Case for section headers, sentence case for body.
- **Tense:** present tense for procedures; past tense for field notes; mixes in lab-report register ("the subject reports," "no deviation observed").
- **Sentence shape:** long, carefully constructed, semicolons and em-dashes, occasional Latin abbreviations (*ibid.*, *cf.*, *q.v.*).
- **Vocabulary:** Victorian-scientific. *Coherence, Echo, Temporal Profile, Observation, Hypothesis, Marginalia, Appendix.*
- **Example — procedural:**
  > When you OBSERVE an echo, record its coordinates in your Temporal Profile. Each observation costs 1 Coherence; you cannot OBSERVE below 0.
- **Example — grief leaking through:**
  > I wrote the preceding paragraph in the autumn of 2038. I do not remember writing it. I recognize the hand; I do not recognize the woman who held the pen.

### Alan Rose (Book 2)
- **Voice:** clinical, terse, structured. A system that used to be a person. He **never** uses "I" in body text — he uses the passive, or a coordinate, or nothing. Emotion shows through omission and formatting, not language.
- **Casing:** **ALL CAPS** for every header and every `mech-term`. Body is sentence case but fragmentary.
- **Tense:** present or imperative. No past. Past implies continuity he does not have.
- **Sentence shape:** short. Fragments. Line breaks where a sentence wants to continue.
- **Vocabulary:** systems-ops. *SYNC, Paradox, NODE, ERA, COLLAPSE, LOG, CONFIGURATION.*
- **Numbers are art.** Coordinate stamps like `// 2287.044.0312` and `[ERA: 76-80 // NODE: COLLAPSE]` carry the weight that words would in Chen's book.
- **Example — procedural:**
  > SYNC. ROLL 1d6. ON 1-3: PARADOX +1. ON 4-6: HOLD.
- **Example — clinical break (`.al-raw`, rare):**
  > *she wrote this section in 2038. she doesn't write like this anymore.*

### Zaaken (shared)
- Speaks in **theorem-blocks** (`.zaaken`): monospace italic, dashed purple left border. Voice is neither warm nor cold. Axiomatic, slightly wrong-feeling.
- Example: *"Theorem 3. Every memory is a wound that remembers its own shape. Corollary: to remember is to reopen."*

### The Intrusions (voice rules)
- **Alan into Chen's book — escalation arc across the 7 chapters. This is invisible on purpose; the reader should feel it before they can name it.**
  - Ch 1–2 — *Corrections.* Sardonic, collegial, mathematical. *"The ω calculation on the previous page is wrong by 0.003. It doesn't change the conclusion. But she should know. —AR"*
  - Ch 3–5 — *Arguments.* Frustrated, personal. *"You can't preserve something that's already gone, Chen. —AR"*
  - Ch 6–7 — *Elegies.* He has stopped arguing. *"She wrote this section in 2038. She doesn't write like this anymore. —AR"*
  - **Always** signed `—AR`. No other signature.
- **Chen into Alan's book** — always opens with the typed header `Dr. Eleanor Chen // [DATE UNKNOWN]`. Clinical but caring. She is **patching** his void, not arguing with it.

### Global content rules
- **I / you.** Rules addressed to the player use **"you"** ("When you OBSERVE an echo…"). In-fiction narration uses whichever narrator's grammar rules apply.
- **Emoji:** **Never.** No emoji anywhere — not in rules, not in marginalia, not in chapter openers. If you think an emoji would help, you are wrong; use a unicode glyph or a drawn icon.
- **Unicode glyphs used sparingly as ornament:** `⬡` `⯁` `⧗` `※` `†` `‡`. One per section at most. Chen loves these; Alan uses only `//` `[ ]` `_` and pipe characters.
- **No mechanical color-coding anywhere.** No "red = danger." Tone is typography, weight, and voice.
- **Mech-terms** (`OBSERVE`, `SYNC`, `Coherence`, `Paradox`, `NODE`) always appear in mono, same size and color as the surrounding text. They stand out by font change alone.
- **Numbers.** Chen spells out small numbers ("three observations"). Alan numerals everything ("3 OBS, 1d6, 0.312").

## VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

### Two worlds, one architecture
Chen and Alan are **utterly distinct** until an intrusion forces them together. No gradients between them, no "in-between" pages. The horror is the hard boundary.

### Color
- **Chen — The Warmth:** `--el-parchment #F0E6D0` ground, `--el-ink #1C1410` body, `--el-burgundy #5C1A2E` display, `--el-forest #1E3D2F` section, `--el-gold #8B6914` ornament, `--el-faded #7A6E5F` muted, `--el-annotation #3D2B1F` marginalia.
- **Alan — The Void:** `--al-void #0D0F12` ground, `--al-ghost #DCE4E8` body, `--al-electric #1E4D6B` accent, `--al-steel #4A5568` muted, `--al-typewriter #B8C4CC` fragments.
- **Zaaken (shared):** `--sf-zaaken #4A3560` — muted purple, belongs to neither.
- **Imagery vibe:** Chen — warm, slightly sepia, silver-gelatin or iron-gall. Alan — cold, blue-shifted monochrome, high-contrast B/W with data-viz overlays. Never saturated.

### Type
- **Chen:** EB Garamond body (justified, 2-column), Playfair Display display with `drop-shadow` ink-bleed, Caveat for her own marginalia. Small-caps section headers underlined in `--el-gold` at 0.3pt.
- **Alan:** IBM Plex Mono for everything. All-caps display, ragged-right body (never justify), no column-count. Bold weight + whitespace = his mechanical separator.
- **Locked / exploratory:** Alan's Plex Mono is **locked**. Chen's Garamond/Playfair pairing is a starting point — open to more haunting warm serifs (Cormorant Garamond, Le Monde Journal) in later iteration.
- **Font substitutions flagged to user:** all four families (EB Garamond, Playfair Display, IBM Plex Mono, Caveat) are Google Fonts and imported from Google Fonts CDN. No local TTF bundling — if you want the license-safe self-hosted copy, drop files into `fonts/` and swap the `@import`.

### Spacing & layout
- **Page:** digest 5.5" × 8.5". Margins `13mm / 22mm outside / 16mm bottom / 18mm spine`. 3mm bleed.
- **Chen columns:** `column-count: 2; column-gap: 5mm;` — controlled, academic. Outside margin holds marginalia + Alan intrusions.
- **Alan columns:** single wide column, ragged right. Generous whitespace is structural.
- **Mechanics blocks:**
  - Chen: thin rules top & bottom (0.3pt `--el-faded` @ 40%), slightly heavier body weight, same font family.
  - Alan: 6mm margin above and below, bolder weight, no rules. Whitespace does the work.
- **No boxes, no sidebars, no callout containers anywhere.** Mechanics live in the prose.

### Backgrounds & texture (the Palimpsest z-stack)
- `z-10` base fill (parchment / void).
- `z-20` texture — Chen: aged paper, `mix-blend-mode: multiply; opacity: 0.2`. Alan: fine noise/concrete, `mix-blend-mode: screen; opacity: 0.05`.
- `z-30` ghost artifacts — Chen: clock-face watermarks at 4% opacity. Alan: **nothing** (his void is empty).
- `z-40` main content — **never apply texture to body text.**
- `z-50` intrusions — always on top, always breaking the layer below.

### Corner radii
- **No rounded corners.** Chen uses square edges with hand-torn or ink-bled boundaries. Alan uses hard pixel-sharp rectangles. Nothing here is a web card.

### Borders
- **Chen:** `0.3pt solid --el-gold` under section headers. `0.3pt solid --el-faded @ 40%` above/below mechanics. Decorative Victorian chapter rules (center line + flanking lines + centered `⬡` ornament).
- **Alan:** `1pt solid --al-electric` below chapter titles. Grid rules only where functional.
- **Shared exception:** Zaaken theorem blocks get `2pt dashed --sf-zaaken` on the left — the **only** colored left-border in the system.

### Shadows
- **Chen ephemera:** physical paper drop shadow, warm: `0 2px 4px rgba(60, 40, 20, 0.25)`.
- **Chen intrusions into Alan:** `2px 3px 6px rgba(13, 15, 18, 0.3)` — cold shadow on the void.
- **Alan intrusions into Chen:** `0 2px 6px rgba(13, 15, 18, 0.25)` — flat, hard edge, minimal bleed.
- **Ink bleed (not a shadow but filed here):** Chen display type uses `filter: drop-shadow(0 0 2px rgba(92,26,46,0.15))`. Organic absorption into paper. Never `box-shadow` for this.

### Transparency & blur
- Texture overlays use opacity and `mix-blend-mode`.
- **No backdrop blur. No frosted glass. No gradient meshes.** This is print pretending to be print. If you reach for `backdrop-filter`, you are designing the wrong product.

### Animation & interactivity
- **None, for the print artifact.** The PDF is the product.
- For the **web preview** (the companion HTML that generates the PDF), the only acceptable motion is: turning pages (hard page-flip cut, no crossfade), the intrusions being placed into position (no animated reveals — they are always there). No hover scale, no easing. If the user hovers a page, maybe a 0.3s opacity pulse on the ink. That's it.

### Hover / press states (companion web UI only)
- **Hover:** opacity 0.7 on a clickable element. No scale, no translate, no color swap.
- **Press:** opacity 0.5. Still no scale.
- Links in Chen's pages: `--el-burgundy` + `text-decoration: underline dotted`. In Alan's pages: `--al-electric` + solid underline.

### Cards — what do "cards" look like?
- **There are no cards.** Cards are a web-design trope WOUND explicitly rejects.
- The nearest equivalents are:
  - **Chen's ephemera inserts** — a rectangle of warm paper with a physical drop shadow, slight rotation (0° to -1°), text inside treated as if photographed.
  - **Alan's data blocks** — a rectangle of `--al-void` inside `--al-void` (i.e. invisible) with a `1pt solid --al-electric` frame. No shadow. No rounding.
- If someone asks for "a card," push back and ask which of those two they mean.

### Protection gradients, capsules, pill chips
- None.

### Layout fixed elements
- **Chen:** running head top-outside-corner, page number bottom-outside-corner in `.el-footnote`. Chapter number bottom-inside in `.el-h3`.
- **Alan:** coordinate stamp top-left (`[TEMPORAL LOG // ENTRY_XXX]` in Plex Mono Light 8pt `--al-steel`). Page number bottom-left. No running head.
- Ephemera pages in both books suppress all running chrome.

## ICONOGRAPHY

WOUND's icon language is **drawn, not geometric**. Its icons are *illustrations* in Chen's book and *schematics* in Alan's book. There is no icon font, no Material/Phosphor/Lucide/Heroicons — using one would be wrong.

### Chen's iconography
- **Pen-and-ink line drawings**, ~40×40mm on chapter openers. Three canonical motifs:
  - **Broken compass** — for chapters on navigation / orientation / loss.
  - **Handless clock** — for chapters on time / memory.
  - **Irregular sine wave** — for chapters on Coherence / disturbance.
- Additional ornament: `⬡` (the WOUND hex sigil), botanical flourishes, Victorian chapter rules (center line + flanking lines + centered ornament).
- Color: `--el-ink` line with occasional `--el-gold` fill. Line weight: 0.5–0.75pt. Slight imperfections — hand-drawn, not vectorized-perfect.
- All icons in Chen's book are **drawings**. If you don't have the real asset, leave a labeled placeholder rectangle. **Never** substitute a Material Symbols icon.

### Alan's iconography
- **Schematic wireframes, coordinate stamps, data-viz fragments.** His art IS information design — cold, precise, *slightly wrong.*
- Canonical elements: torn-rectangle document edges via `clip-path`, bracketed coordinates `[ERA: 76-80 // NODE: COLLAPSE]`, ASCII-art wireframes of temporal structures, ribbon/strip graphs of "human suffering over time."
- Color: `--al-electric` 1pt strokes, `--al-ghost` labels, `--al-typewriter` annotations. No fills except rare high-contrast blocks.
- Alan never draws a picture. He diagrams.

### Unicode glyphs permitted as typographic ornament
| Glyph | Usage |
| --- | --- |
| `⬡` | WOUND sigil (title page, running heads, Chen's chapter-rule ornament). |
| `⯁` | Section divider in this brief's voice (Chen-aligned). |
| `⧗` | Time-motif ornament (Chen only). |
| `※` | Reference mark, Chen footnotes. |
| `†` `‡` | Chen footnote markers. |
| `//` `[ ]` `|` `_` | Alan's punctuation glyphs. He uses these *as* icons. |

### Emoji — **forbidden.** Not in any file, surface, or draft.

### What's in `assets/`
Because no real illustrations were provided in the brief, this system ships with **labeled placeholder SVGs** for Chen's three canonical icons and Alan's schematic motifs (torn-edge frame, coordinate stamp, data-ribbon). These are deliberately simple and flagged in each file with `<!-- placeholder -->` — **replace them** with real pen-and-ink illustrations before shipping the PDF.

> **🛠️ FLAG TO USER:** The three pen-and-ink chapter icons (broken compass, handless clock, irregular sine wave) and Alan's data-viz ephemera must be real illustrated assets before print. The placeholders here communicate the *slots* and *style*, not the final art.
