Section I

1. Overview

The CTH Glyph System is a constructed writing language used throughout the WOUND duology. It encodes the physics of Conjugate Temporal Harmonics — selfhood, time, memory, fracture — as a finite set of letterforms that compose into words, sentences, and equations. In the fiction, chronotech weapons inscribe these glyphs to manipulate the Chronoverse; in the book, they appear as chapter sigils, mechanic markers, oracle stamps, and faction signatures.

The system rests on three commitments. It is decomposable — every conceptual glyph reduces to a readable arrangement of atomic strokes; a player who learns the alphabet can decode any inscription. It is generative — the chronotech, the player, and the designer can combine atomics into new compositions that mean exactly what their decomposition says they mean. It is operational — the CTH equations are not metaphors. The equations are statements written in this language, and inscribing them produces effects in the Chronoverse.

The aesthetic reference is industrial-runic: weighty geometric letterforms, distinct silhouettes at thumbnail size, no decoration beyond what the grammar requires. The system trades the occult-astrological feel of a single-template sigil set for the typographic clarity of a real alphabet.

Section II

2. The Five Design Laws

Every glyph in the system obeys these laws. Any new addition to the corpus must obey them.

LawRuleWhy
1Atomic primacy. Every glyph is either an atomic stroke or a readable composition of atomic strokes.Decomposability. A player who learns 11 letters can read 12 words and infinitely many sentences.
2Silhouette distinction. Every glyph must be uniquely identifiable at thumbnail size (24×24 px minimum).Inline use in margins, on character sheets, in oracle tables. If you can't tell them apart small, the language fails at use.
3Weighty geometric stroke. 5-unit stroke weight on a 100-unit canvas. Square line caps. Miter joins. No flourish.Krakoan-industrial register. The glyphs are technical, not mystical. Production scaling requires consistency.
4Composition by overlap or sequence. Glyphs combine either by overlapping into a unified mark (compound) or by reading side-by-side as a word (sequence).Two composition modes is enough. More creates ambiguity; fewer prevents the language from encoding the equations.
5Dialect by uniform transform. Each faction's dialect is one rule applied to every stroke. No bespoke faction redrawings.Any glyph × any dialect renders automatically. The four factions can sign any inscription in their hand without new artwork.
Construction grid: All glyphs render on a 100×100 viewBox. Default stroke weight is 5 units; auxiliary marks (dialect ticks, sub-strokes) use 2–3 units. stroke-linecap="square" and stroke-linejoin="miter" are mandatory — round caps soften the industrial register.

Section III

3. The Atomic Alphabet

Eleven strokes. These are the writing system at its foundation. Every composition in Section 5, every equation in Section 7, and every dialect inscription in Section 8 reduces to a sequence of these eleven forms.

τ

Tau · Time

The axis of time, displacement, the spine of selfhood. Appears in any concept involving temporal motion or persistence.

Horizontal bar (22,22)→(78,22); vertical descending to (50,82)

μ

Mu · Mass

Emotional mass, narrative weight, the gravitational charge of an event. Two stacked bars; reads as accumulated weight.

Two horizontal bars at y=38 and y=62

Ψ

Psi · Waveform

Identity as wave-state. The trident form encodes three converging modes resolving into a single carrier. The most complex atomic.

Three prongs at x=28/50/72 from y=18 to y=46; crossbar; central descent to y=84

I

Iota · Intervention

Choice, agency, deliberate action. The left-facing bracket. Pairs with C in equations where intent and consequence must both appear.

Left bracket: vertical x=38 from y=20 to y=80, with short top and bottom hooks rightward to x=58

C

Causality

Consequence, resistance, what the world pushes back with. The right-facing bracket. Always mirrors an I in equations of action and response.

Mirror of Iota: vertical x=62, hooks leftward to x=42

Ω

Omega · Field

The chronofield. The boundary inside which CTH variables interact. The only true circle in the alphabet — its uniqueness signals its scope.

Circle, center (50,50), radius 32

Δ

Delta · Fracture

Change as breakage. The operator of transformation. Sharp apex, miter joins — points upward as a directional vector of disruption.

Triangle, apex (50,18), base (22,80)–(78,80)

ω

Omega-mini · Oscillation

Micro-oscillation, vibration, the unit of frequency. The only true curve in the alphabet. Distinct from Ω by stroke type, not closure.

Bezier wave: M(18,65) Q(34,28)(50,50) Q(66,72)(82,35)

S

Sigma · Limit

Collapse threshold, terminal state, the edge below which a system cannot operate. Inverse of Δ — points downward, miter apex at bottom.

Inverted-V polyline: (22,28)→(50,78)→(78,28)

R

Rho · Mirror

Recursion, repetition, the operator of reflection. Two parallel verticals. Doubling R produces RECURSION — mirror of mirror.

Two parallels at x=35 and x=65, full height y=18 to y=82

E

Epsilon · Erasure

Void, null, what was removed. The dashed horizontal stroke. Reads as absence-with-trace — something was there, and is no longer.

Dashed horizontal at y=50, stroke-dasharray "8 8"

Section IV

4. Composition Grammar

Atomics combine in two modes. Mode is determined by the relationship being expressed.

Mode A — Compound (overlap)

When two atomics describe a single unified state — a bounded thing, a state-with-modifier — they overlap into one mark. ANCHOR is τ bounded by Ω — time inside field — and these overlap because the boundedness is part of the concept, not a sequence of actions. COLLAPSE is Ω with Δ slashing through it — the field is being broken, in one moment, as one act.

Compound example: ANCHOR

+
→

Ω + τ → ANCHOR · "time bounded by field"

Mode B — Sequence (side-by-side)

When two atomics describe a process — a thing acting on another thing, a thing repeated, a thing followed by an outcome — they read side-by-side as a word. ECHO is Ψ R: waveform passed through a mirror. RECURSION is R R: mirror of mirror. Both are operations applied in time, not unified states.

Sequence example: ECHO

→

Ψ R → ECHO · "waveform passed through mirror"

Choosing the mode

The test is grammatical, not aesthetic: is the relationship between the two atomics simultaneous (compound) or sequential (sequence)? "Time inside field" is simultaneous — it has to be inside it for it to mean anything, in one instant. "Waveform passed through mirror" is sequential — the wave existed, then was reflected, then exists as something altered. When in doubt, default to compound for unified states and sequence for processes.

Operators

When writing equations (Section 7), four operators bridge between glyphs and standard math notation:

=Equality — used in equations
×Multiplication / convolution
/Division / per-unit
f( )Function-of, applied to bracketed glyph string

Operators render in monospace serif at body size. They are not glyphs — they are typographic punctuation that joins glyphs into statements. The glyphs are the nouns and verbs; the operators are the conjunctions.

Section VI

6. The Master Sigil

The Master Sigil contains every CTH variable bounded by the field. It is the unified equation written as a single mark: τ, μ, Ψ, I, C, all inside Ω.

τ + μ + Ψ + I + C bounded by Ω

Because the Master Sigil contains the entire alphabet, it is the only inscription that cannot be reduced further. It is the upper bound of what the language can encode in one mark. The chronotech does not inscribe it because inscribing it would mean producing the entire CTH equation simultaneously — which is what the First Scar was.

Production rule: The Master Sigil appears in WOUND exactly three times. Cover (intact). emiT title page (one terminal missing — first sign of damage). Saul Files closing page (one terminal replaced by the MEMORY glyph — the cycle made visible). Every additional appearance dilutes the move. Treat the sigil like a load-bearing word: used sparingly, never decorated.

Section VIII

8. Faction Dialects

Each of the four factions writes the same alphabet differently. The dialect is one transformation applied uniformly to every stroke. Same letters, same words, different scripts — the way the same English sentence can appear in serif Times, monospace Courier, blackletter Old English, and handwritten cursive without changing meaning.

The rule below demonstrates each dialect on Ψ (the most complex atomic, so the rule is most visible). The same transformation applies to every other atomic and every composition, automatically.

Base Ψ

No transformation

Authority

Every stroke doubled

Church

Cross-mark on every terminus

Scionfall

Every stroke dashed

Chronoclasts

15° clockwise rotation

Generalization: Any glyph can render in any dialect by wrapping its body in the transformation rule. The pipeline is: base glyph SVG → apply dialect transform → output dialect SVG. No bespoke per-faction artwork is required for any composition. Each dialect adds approximately 1 line of code to the asset pipeline.

Section IX

9. Writing in CTH

Composing new concepts

A concept the system does not yet name can be written by composing atomics. The procedure:

  1. State the concept in plain English. Example: "the memory of a fracture."
  2. Identify the atomic components. Memory = Ψ ω; fracture = Δ. The concept therefore involves three atomics.
  3. Determine the relationship. Is the fracture inside the memory (compound), or did the memory follow the fracture (sequence)? If the former, render as MEMORY with Δ overlapped. If the latter, render as Δ followed by MEMORY.
  4. Test the inscription. If a reader who knows the alphabet can recover the original concept by decomposition, the inscription is valid. If they cannot, the composition is ambiguous and needs refinement.

Writing equations

Equations are sequences of glyphs joined by operators. The glyphs are the variables; the operators (=, ×, /, f( )) are typographic punctuation. To write a new equation:

  1. State the relationship in standard math. Example: "the rate of paradox accumulation equals burden times intervention."
  2. Replace each variable with its glyph composition. ΔPARADOX = BURDEN × I, with PARADOX rendered as Ψ-through-Δ and BURDEN as triple-μ.
  3. Render glyphs at uniform size (40px standard for inline equations; 56px for displayed equations).
  4. Set operators in monospace serif at 1.3rem, vertically centered with the glyphs.

The chronotech inscription

In-fiction, the chronotech weaponizes the alphabet by inscribing glyph sequences directly onto matter, energy, or waveforms. The inscription is the operation: writing ANCHOR onto an object bounds that object to its time-coordinate; writing COLLAPSE onto a field forces field failure; writing the Wave Equation onto a retrieval cuff stabilizes the wearer.

The chronotech does not interpret the inscription. The inscription executes itself by being inscribed. This is why the equations must be precisely written: a missing terminal on a Ψ doesn't render the inscription harmless; it renders it different. Loop Authority's Doctrine 7.4.b governs which atomics may be inscribed by which rank of operator. Most field operatives can inscribe single-atomic protections (Ω alone for warding; τ alone for navigation). Only Senior Loopwrights are licensed for full equation inscriptions.

Production checklist before layout

  1. Verify all 28 SVG assets render correctly in your target software (Typst, InDesign, Affinity Publisher).
  2. Confirm stroke="currentColor" is respected by your renderer; if not, replace with explicit fill hex before deployment.
  3. Test thumbnail-size legibility: every glyph at 24px should still be identifiable. If two glyphs blur into each other at that size, the system has failed Law 2.
  4. Test the dialect transform pipeline: produce ANCHOR in all four dialects and confirm the same rule applies cleanly to a complex composition.
  5. Lock the Master Sigil to its three sacred locations only. Any other appearance dilutes the move.
