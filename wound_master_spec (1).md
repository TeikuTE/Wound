# WOUND Master Layout Spec
*Footnote placements and glyph placements, anchored to passages.*

## How to use this document

This is the single source of truth for where every Finder footnote and every CTH glyph goes in the WOUND duology. Passages are quoted directly from the V12 manuscript so the spec stays valid through any pagination shift.

For each location, the spec lists:
- **The anchor passage** — quoted from V12. Find this passage in the manuscript; place the listed elements relative to it.
- **Glyphs to place** — chapter sigil, inline margin marker, or specific composition.
- **Footnotes to place** — the Finder note with its full text, numbered as in `finder_notes_v1.md`.
- **Visual notes** — color shifts, escalation stages, dialect variants where they apply.

**Three structural rules:**

1. **Color progression on the Finder's notes** shifts in three discrete jumps, not gradually. Acts I–II are #6B6657 mid-gray. Acts III–IV are #6B3232 burgundy. Act V is near-void burgundy with Caveat contamination. The shifts are signposted below at the exact passages where they begin.

2. **Glyph escalation** applies to PARADOX, MEMORY, and ANCHOR only — three load-bearing concepts that degrade across the book. The stage at which to render them is specified at each placement. All other glyphs render at Stage 0 (clean) always.

3. **Late-insertion items** (notes 57–60 and four ghost glyphs) physically exist on early pages but are *placed last in production*, after Acts I–IV are typeset, and rendered at 99% saturation of the standard color so a sharp-eyed re-reader senses something off without being able to locate it.

# PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

Before going to print:

1. **Verify the Saul thread.** Note 10 ("Named my Echo Saul"), Note 40 (the rain-on-asphalt corruption), Note 53 (the Saul Files reveal), Note 59 (the late-insertion inversion). Saul appears nowhere else in the apparatus — no test characters named Saul in oracle results, no Saul in mutation examples. Verify before layout lock.

2. **Verify the Loop Authority illustration on Footnote 31's "page 54" reference.** Either pin the Authority illustration to whichever page becomes 54 in final pagination, or update Footnote 31 to point to wherever the illustration lands. This is the only footnote where pagination matters.

3. **Verify Master Sigil appears exactly three times.** Cover (intact); emiT title (one terminal missing); Saul Files closing (one terminal replaced by MEMORY). Nowhere else, including the back cover, marketing materials, or any apparatus.

4. **Verify the color shift at Footnote 25.** The Finder's footnote color must visibly change from mid-gray to burgundy starting with Footnote 25. The shift is the visual signal that Act III has begun.

5. **Verify late-insertion items render at 99% saturation.** Notes 57–60 and the four late-insertion glyphs must be visibly present but slightly off in tone from the standard footnotes around them.

6. **Verify the p38 dialect ANCHOR spread.** Four faction dialect ANCHORs side-by-side in Chapter VI Crisis Management is the visual centerpiece of the book. If the four don't read as same-concept-four-attitudes at a glance, the dialect system has failed Law 2 of the glyph brief.

7. **Verify the Apikoros/Anachron diegetic logic.** Note 1 has the Finder coining "Apikoros." Note 27 reveals the Hebrew meaning. Note 60 (late-insertion on cover) restores them as the published titles. The chain must hold across the book's chronology.
